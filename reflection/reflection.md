# Application: Wysa

- Most important difference between pipelines:
The manual pipeline produced more precise and context-aware personas and requirements because they were carefully derived manually from specific reviews.  The automated pipeline was faster but generated broader and sometimes vague outputs.  The hybrid pipeline improved upon this by manually refining the automated results, making them clearer and more grounded in evidence.

- Most useful pipeline:
The hybrid pipeline was the most useful because it combined the efficiency of automation with human judgment.  It retained the structure and speed of the automated pipeline while improving clarity, specificity, and traceability.

- Most surprising finding:
Many automated requirements appeared correct at a glance but were too vague to be effectively tested (e.g., "user-friendly" or "affordable").  This showed that grammatically correct output is not always suitable for requirements engineering.

- Observed weakness in the automated pipeline:
The automated pipeline sometimes produced personas and requirements that included unsupported assumptions or overly generic statements.  It also struggled with ambiguity, which led to weaker acceptance criteria and less precise validation tests.
